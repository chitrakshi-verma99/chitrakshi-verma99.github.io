Identity, Values and Strengths

I have been in hospitality since I was 19, working as a full-time chef. When i was living in Wellington,I made many friends, most of them were travellers. I had a best friend who was also a chef, And the person ended up opening their own Eatery
on Cuba street. I left my 2-Hat-Restaurent job, and ended up working for my friend as a Sous-chef. I was managing a staff
of 10 people. During these times , the relationship of An Owner and an employee was coming in the way of friendship.
We had differences in deciding the Menu's and ended up in an argument. At that moment I had a hard decision to make
choosing between my happy professional life and a friendship that is no longer beneficial or healthy.
I choose my happiness as I respect my self and knew I deserved better. I changed my job and felt way more satisfied on a professional end.


I grew up in a country with a diverse culture and highly traditional people. My indian background influnced me a lot growing up defining most of my values and me as an Individual. I was made to believe things that no one knows in the outside world. Yet, it was all family and tradition. Now that I live away from my birth country and family, I have seen a drastic change in how I am adaptible to my surroundings and how I settled in a new country all these years. Honestly, If I move back to New Delhi, I wouldn't be the same person or would have a bit difficulty changing back to my older ways! My point of veiw of looking at life has changed,So I do not think I can follow my family traditions 100% now.
